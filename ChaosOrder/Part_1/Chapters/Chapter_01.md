# Chapter 1: The Match

"Ball toss. Footwork," Kate said, [I want clean rhythm.] She rolled her shoulders once; the grip felt tacky against her palm. Heat sat heavy on her neck. The ball rose, a pale coin. She tracked the seam and snapped through.

The serve kissed the tape and drifted long.

"Fine," Kate said, [Not today.] as the line judge lifted a flat hand. The point slipped like sweat down her spine.

"Reset," Kate said, [I hate this shake.] She bounced on her toes, legs humming, calves tight. Breath in, out, in.

Mackenzie adjusted her visor. The brim cut a neat shadow over her eyes. "Ready?" Mackenzie said, [Stay steady.] She tapped her strings once and settled.

"Play," Kate said, [I want the point.] She set her feet.

The rally stretched. Thwack. Skid. Squeak. Kate drove a crosscourt forehand deep; Mackenzie rolled a high slice back, slow and floating.

"Short," Kate said, [Take it.] She stepped in, shoulders coiled.

The forehand went for the line and missed by an inch.

"Out," the line judge said.

"Game, set, match," the umpire said. The applause came late and loud, edges hard.

"Great match," Mackenzie said at the net. She leaned in and tapped racquets. [Hold gracious.]

"Sure," Kate said, [I wanted that one.] She kept the smile steady, teeth together, eyes bright.

"See you at regionals," Mackenzie said, [She bites at lines.] She turned light on her feet, visor already straight.

"Later," Kate said. [I need a wall.] She walked off into the baked air, each step measured.

Kate sat on the bench and tugged the tape from her fingers. "Hydrate," Kate said, [I hurt.] She drained the last of her bottle and closed her eyes a second too long.

Kate thumbed the locket at her collarbone. "Hi," Kate said, [I miss you.] She clicked it shut and let the weight sit warm against her skin.

"Quick update," Kate said to her front camera, [Don’t show the shake.] She lifted the phone and found the light. "Not my day. Footwork got sloppy. We work, we move. See you at practice." Kate smiled through the last word and tapped save. [Keep them with me.]

The locker room smelled like disinfectant and wet grip tape. "Five minutes," Kate said, [Get it together.] She washed her face with cold water and watched the drip line on her jaw.

"Hey." A voice from the doorway. Mackenzie. "Tough one," Mackenzie said, [Keep it clean.] She held a towel out.

"Thanks," Kate said, [Don’t need your towel.] She took it anyway, pressed it to her face, and handed it back.

"You going to the boys’ scrimmage?" Mackenzie said, [Gauge the draw.] She leaned on the jamb, easy.

"Maybe," Kate said, [No.] She zipped her bag and stood. "Footwork drills first."

"See you out there," Mackenzie said, [She’ll grind.] She left without a squeak from her shoes.

The sun had dipped a shade when Kate stepped outside. "Cooler," Kate said, [Still heavy.] She crossed toward the fence, shoes ticking on the concrete.

"You good?" A freshman stood there with a water bottle and a nervous half-smile. "I’m Lena," the girl said.

"Always," Kate said, [I ache.] She took the water and nodded. "Footwork first. Always. Remember that," Kate said, [Control is mine.] She handed the bottle back.

"Footwork," Lena said. She nodded like it was a password.

"Learn names," Kate said, [No more blanks.] She shouldered her bag.

The rectangle of bleachers where Grace should be sat empty in the glare. "Later," Grace always said. "Later," Kate said to the silent seats, [I want you here.] She stopped looking.

A minivan rolled up, soft green and familiar. The passenger window slid down. "Rescue mission," Tracy said, [Please get in.] She grinned and shoved the door open.

"You are a saint," Kate said, [I need the ride.] She climbed in. The AC hit her skin and wiped the court off in one sweep.

"I brought cookies," Tracy said. She held a warm paper bag between them. "Recovery. Science."

"Science," Kate said, [Sugar now.] She broke a cookie and took a bite. The crack gave, then the butter melted, cinnamon bright on her tongue.

"You okay?" Tracy said, [Tell me real.] She watched Kate, braid slipping over her shoulder.

"Obviously," Kate said, [I’m not fine.] She chewed, shoulders dropping a notch.

"You can be both," Tracy said, [I’ve got you.] She pushed the bag closer.

"How’s the bakery?" Kate said, [Change the subject.] She brushed a crumb from her knee.

"Busy," Tracy said. "Dad made a new sourdough. Mom says he’s flirting with the yeast."

"Gross," Kate said, [Good gross.] The laugh came small and honest.

The courts shrank in the mirror as the van rolled out. "Post or save?" Kate said, [Don’t watch it again.] She thumbed her phone. The thumbnail froze her mid-smile.

"Save," Tracy said, [No need to perform.] She tapped the dash lightly to a song only she heard.

Kate locked the screen and leaned back. The locket pressed a half-moon into her skin. The bracelet chimed as the van took a pothole. "Next time," Kate said, [I want the win.]

"Next time," Tracy said, "cupcakes anyway."

"Balance," Kate said, [Hold steady.] She let her eyes close for one breath and kept her grip loose.

— — —

"Beam set," Melissa said, [Don’t rush.] She lifted onto releve and found the line. The gym air held chalk and old music.

"Again," Isabella said from the side, [Light hands.] She clapped once, sharp.

Melissa landed, heels soft. "One more," Melissa said, [Make it clean.] She reset without looking over.

Isabella watched her daughter’s ankles roll through, careful and strong. "Good," Isabella said, [Stay safe.] She nodded and let the silence be praise.

— — —

Back home, the light leaned warm across Kate’s floor. "Shower," Kate said, [Scrub the day.] She twisted the tap to cold and stood under it until her breath steadied.

"Homework," Kate said, [Later.] She toweled her hair and sat on the edge of the bed.

The phone buzzed. Mackenzie’s name lit the screen. "Good to see your speed today," the text said.

"Thanks," Kate typed, [I hate that I needed that.] "Regionals soon."

"See you there," Mackenzie replied, [She won’t give me space.] A tennis ball emoji bounced back.

Kate set the phone face down. "Stretch," Kate said, [Stay ready.] She folded forward and reached, hamstrings pulling cleanly.

"Tomorrow," Kate said, [Work.] She lay back, the locket warm where it rested, and watched the ceiling until the day thinned out of her chest.
