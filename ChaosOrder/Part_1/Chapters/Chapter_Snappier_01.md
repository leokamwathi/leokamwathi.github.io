# Chapter 1: The Match (Triple_T snappier)

Alarm punched six a.m. right in the ear. Kate flailed for snooze, missed, and squinted at the red digits.

[Two hours of sleep. Vlog gobbled the rest.]

Last night’s edit her pep talk about hard work paying off—had eaten midnight: cuts, captions, a thumbnail grin that lied less than most.

[Work harder, win harder. Cute slogan. Also: ow.]

Phone buzz. Mom: “Work emergency. Had to go in early.” [Figures.]

Kate thumbed a reply: “Hope to see you at the game today. <3” [She wouldn't show] and tossed the phone onto the duvet.

[Manifesting bleachers Mom.]

Downstairs, cereal became a science experiment in a too‑warm bowl. She opened her channel.

[Eight likes already. Go me.]

One comment from Tracy: “Heart it <3.”

[Heart you back.]

Today was the last match of the tournament. Finals. Kate vs Mackenzie.

[All roads lead to visor girl.]

She slung her bag, snagged her locket, and stepped into the morning like it was watching.

—

“Ball toss. Footwork.” Kate bounced on her toes, rolled her shoulders, and sent the ball up like a coin flip.

[Heads: ace. Tails: drama.]

She snapped through. The serve kissed the tape… and sailed long.

“Fine.” She squinted at the line judge’s flat hand. [Not today, then.]

“Reset.” Bounce‑bounce. Breath. Grip. Go.

Mackenzie tipped her visor, all tidy and terrifying. “Ready?” [I was born ready.]

“Play.” Feet set. Eyes bright.

Rally on: thwack, skid, squeak. Kate hammered crosscourt; Mackenzie sent back a dreamy slice like a lazy cloud.

“Short.” Kate stepped in, coiled, smacked—just wide.

“Out,” said the judge.

“Game, set, match,” the ump declared. Applause fell like late rain.

Mackenzie met her at the net. Tap‑tap. “Great match.” [Too close]

“Sure.” Kate smiled with all her teeth. [Wanted that one.]

“Next time, K,” Mackenzie said, visor laser‑straight. [Need to work harder.]

“Next time, Kenzie.” [Must work harder.] Kate muttered, heading off, steps neat, shoulders pretending not to sulk.

Bench. Tape off. “Hydrate.” [Everything hurts, pride hurt, Mom no show.]

She drained the bottle, closed her eyes one beat too long, then thumbed the locket at her collarbone.

“Hi,” she whispered. [Miss you.]

Phone up. “Quick update,” she told the front camera. “Not my day. Footwork got sloppy. We’ll fix it. See you at practice.” Save. [Keep them with me. Not the meltdown.]

Locker room: disinfectant chic, wet grip tape aroma. “Five minutes,” she told herself, splashing cold water.

“Hey.” Tracy appeared in the doorway with a towel. “Tough one.” [Keep it clean. No pity.]

“Thanks,” Kate said, pressing and handing it back.

“You almost had her,” Tracy said. [She’s always hard on herself.]

“My footwork still sucks,” Kate said. “I need more practice.” [How much harder.]

“I know what you need first,” Tracy said, opening her bag. “Passion cupcake.” [Her fav]

“You know me too well.” Kate took the cupcake and, for a moment, forgot the match and the empty bleachers. “Thanks, Trixy” [Glad I have you.]

--

They left the locker room together, Tracy matching Kate’s pace down the hall.

A freshman hovered with a water bottle and a nervous smile. “I’m Lena. Are you okay?”

“Yeah, I’m okay. Thanks.” Kate took a sip and handed it back. “Start with footwork. It’ll save you more than anything.” [Control is mine.]

“Footwork,” Lena echoed, like a secret password.

Tracy offered a small wave. “Hey, Lena. I’m Tracy. We’ll be around.”

“Learn names,” Kate told herself, adjusting her strap. “Nice to meet you, Lena.” [No more blanks.]

Bleachers: empty where Grace should be.

“Later,” Grace always said.

“Later,” Kate answered the seats. [I want you here.]

Soft‑green minivan nosed up. Window down. Tracy’s grin: “Rescue mission.” [Get in, silly.]

“You’re a saint,” Kate said, climbing in. The AC erased the court in one icy sweep.

“I brought cookies,” Tracy announced. Warm paper bag, cinnamon halo. “Recovery. Science.”

“Science,” Kate agreed, breaking one. [Sugar now. Feelings later.]

“You okay?” Tracy asked, braid slouching over her shoulder.

“Obviously,” Kate said, with a half‑smile. [Not fine.]

“You can be both,” Tracy said, nudging the bag closer. [I’ve got you.]

“How’s the bakery?” Kate asked.

“Busy. Dad’s flirting with the yeast.”

“Gross,” Kate snorted. [Good gross.]

Courts shrank in the mirror. “Post or save?” Kate asked, thumb hovering. [Do not rewatch.]

“Save,” Tracy said, drumming a private beat on the dash. [No performance necessary.]

Locket pressed a half‑moon. Bracelet chimed on a pothole.

“Next time,” Kate said. [Want the win.]

“Next time,” Tracy promised. “Cupcakes anyway.”

“Balance,” Kate said. [Hold steady.]

One long breath. Grip loose.

— — —

“Beam set,” Melissa said, lifting onto relevé. [Don’t rush it, legs.]

“Again,” Isabella clapped once from the side. [Light hands. Big heart.]

Melissa landed soft‑heeled. “One more.” [Make it clean.]

Isabella watched ankles roll strong. “Good.” [Stay safe.]

— — —

Home light slanted warm. “Shower,” Kate decreed, twisting to cold. [Scrub the day off.]

“Homework,” she told the room. [Later.]

Buzz. Text from Mackenzie: Good speed today.

“Thanks,” Kate typed. [Hated needing that.]

Regionals soon.

“See you there,” Mackenzie replied. Tennis ball emoji. [No space given.]

Phone facedown. “Stretch.” Fold, reach. [Stay ready.]

“Tomorrow.” She lay back, locket warm. [Work.]

Ceiling quieted while the day thinned out of her chest.
