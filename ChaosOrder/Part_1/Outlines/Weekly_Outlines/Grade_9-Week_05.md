# Grade_9 — Week_05

- Dates: 2023-09-25 → 2023-10-01
- Anchor: W.E.I.R.D Tour Day turns curiosity into exposure, while Aki's birthday quietly tests comfort, affection, and who still feels safe.
- Week Summary: Week 5 moves the story into a more explicit technology-and-feelings overlap. The admirer poem from Sept 22 is still fresh enough to color how June reads attention, but the week does not let her hide inside that mystery alone. W.E.I.R.D Tour Day places the cast inside the town's most futuristic institution and turns questions of usefulness, ambition, secrecy, and belonging into something physical and public. Dex gets a week where his strengths are clearly legible, which is good for him and also risky. June has to navigate both curiosity and unease as the world around her starts feeling more designed than natural. Kate confronts what it means to perform poise in a space built around excellence and status. Tracy's thread stays grounded in care, but Aki's birthday makes that care more relational and quietly revealing. The result should be a week where the cast feels pulled toward future versions of themselves they are not yet ready to name.

## Week Flow

- Monday: the group is still carrying soccer-game and admirer-poem fallout, but school routines force them to keep moving.
- Tuesday and Wednesday: W.E.I.R.D Tour Day prep shifts attention toward technology, ambition, and who seems to fit naturally inside that world.
- Thursday: the tour itself becomes the week's main event and pushes Dex, June, and Kate into different kinds of visibility.
- Friday: social fallout from the tour bleeds into rehearsal and private conversation.
- Saturday: Aki's birthday creates a softer emotional beat that reframes Tracy's comfort zone and the shape of her relationships.
- Sunday: the week settles into open questions rather than resolution, especially around the admirer poem and what the W.E.I.R.D visit awakened in different people.

## Main Stories

### MS1 — Guided Futures

- Day/Date: Thu 2023-09-28
- Location: W.E.I.R.D facility lobby, innovation wing, and observation deck
- Focus: Dexter
- Cast: June, Prita, Kate, Alex, school guide, Dr. Sato from W.E.I.R.D, rotating classmates
- Hook: The W.E.I.R.D tour drops Dex into the one school-related environment where his intelligence is immediately legible and rewarded, which changes the social balance of the group for a day.
- Stakes: Belonging, ego, future ambition, and whether Dex can let himself be seen as exceptional without retreating from the people who do not share his comfort there.
- Summary: This is a strong Dex week because the environment validates him before he has to prove himself. He understands the systems, asks the right questions, and receives attention from adults that feels flattering rather than suspicious. That should make him glow a little. The scene works because that glow is double-edged. June feels both proud of him and slightly left behind by the ease with which he fits the space. Kate reads the tour through status and opportunity, which creates a different kind of tension. Prita becomes a natural ally inside the institutional logic of the place, while Alex stays more emotionally skeptical. Dex does well here, but the deeper question is whether being valued by W.E.I.R.D makes him feel more connected to his friends or more tempted to self-sort into a safer, smarter category.
- DC: Medium
- MemoryTag: +weird-fit
- Playtest Outcome: Success With Cost. Dex thrives and is noticed by adults, but that easy fit subtly separates him from the group's emotional rhythm.

### MS3 — Excellence Tour

- Location: W.E.I.R.D presentation hall spillover and school bus ride back
- Focus: Kate
- Cast: Jayden, Mackenzie, Dex, teacher chaperone, two admiring classmates
- Hook: The tour's prestige atmosphere gives Kate a setting where she should thrive, but the experience also exposes how much of her confidence still depends on performance rather than conviction.

Dex's ease here also matters, because he belongs for reasons deeper than style. - DC: Medium
- MemoryTag: +prestige-mirror
- Playtest Outcome: Partial. Kate performs well in the space, but the tour leaves her less certain that prestige equals a future she truly wants.
