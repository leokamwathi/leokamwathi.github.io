# Grade_9 — Week_04

- Dates: 2023-09-18 → 2023-09-24
- Anchor: The first soccer game of the season turns pressure, loyalty, and public performance into the week's main engine.
- Week Summary: Week 4 shifts the spotlight from image to allegiance. After Picture Day, the band has a clearer identity, but the group still has not learned how to survive moments when one member's outside life becomes everybody else's logistical and emotional problem. The first soccer game puts Martin at the center of school attention and forces the rest of the cast to decide what support actually looks like when schedules, pride, and hidden feelings all collide. June and Dex keep trying to move the band forward, but both are forced to confront that momentum is not the same as unity. Kate has to navigate a public event where she knows exactly how to perform social poise and no longer fully trusts it. Alex and June continue the lighthouse thread in smaller beats, letting the mystery deepen through atmosphere instead of plot spikes. Tracy and Nikole carry the gentler emotional stabilizers of the week, which keeps the story rooted in care even while competitiveness rises.
- Calendar beats in play: Sept 22 First Soccer Game of the Season; Sept 22 first secret admirer poem in June's locker.

## Week Flow

- Monday: the group tries to build on Picture Day momentum, but Martin's game schedule immediately changes the week's center of gravity.
- Tuesday and Wednesday: rehearsal plans, team obligations, and social positioning create low-grade conflict around time, loyalty, and expectation.
- Thursday: June and Alex pull one more quiet thread on the lighthouse mystery while everyone else braces for the game.
- Friday: the first soccer game becomes the week's public stress event, while June also receives the first secret admirer poem, adding a separate hidden-message thread to the week.
- Weekend: fallout lands in smaller, more personal scenes that clarify which bonds are becoming dependable.

## Main Stories

### MS1 — Split Schedule

- Day/Date: Mon 2023-09-18
- Location: Waterfall High, rehearsal room and hallway outside the gym
- Focus: June
- Cast: Dex, Tracy, Martin, Alex, Ms. Vale, Kate listening more than she speaks
- Hook: Just as the band starts feeling coherent, Martin's game-week obligations disrupt the rehearsal schedule and force the group to decide how flexible they actually are.
- Stakes: Band momentum, group trust, and whether June can keep the project moving without treating people like interchangeable parts.
- Summary: June begins the week wanting to capitalize on the fragile coherence the group found after Picture Day. Instead, she runs straight into the first real test of whether the band can survive competing commitments. Martin cannot simply drop soccer, and once that becomes clear, June has to choose between frustration and adaptation. Dex leans toward efficiency, Tracy toward accommodation, and Alex refuses to let the energy turn sterile. Kate does not dominate the scene, but her relative quiet matters because she has begun watching the band more carefully than she comments on it. The emotional core is June realizing that building something real means designing around other people's burdens, not just around her own vision.
- DC: Medium
- MemoryTag: +schedule-adaptation
- Playtest Outcome: Partial Success. June keeps the band moving, but only after giving up the idea that momentum matters more than people's outside lives.

### MS2 — Captain In The Making

- Day/Date: Wed 2023-09-20
- Location: Waterfall High, soccer field during practice and bench area at dusk
- Focus: Martin
- Cast: Coach Alvarez, Malcolm, teammates, Kate arriving late with Jayden nearby
- Hook: Coach publicly leans on Martin as an emotional center for the team before the first game, turning praise into obligation.
- Stakes: Martin's sense of self, his loyalty to the team, and whether he keeps accepting a future other people keep handing him.
- Summary: Martin has been feeling pressure for weeks, but this is the first scene where it becomes visibly institutional. Coach Alvarez does not simply tell him to play well. He frames him as the kind of person other people are supposed to follow. That kind of praise is dangerous because it sounds generous while narrowing his options. Malcolm's presence sharpens the competitive atmosphere, not by attacking Martin, but by embodying a cleaner relationship to ambition than Martin himself has. Kate arriving late with Jayden nearby adds a second layer of performance; Martin is suddenly being watched from multiple social directions at once. The scene should end with Martin looking more composed than he feels, which is exactly why it matters.
- DC: Hard
- MemoryTag: +game-pressure
- Playtest Outcome: Partial/Fail. Martin looks steady enough for Coach Alvarez to lean on him even more, but the praise lands as pressure and deepens his identity conflict instead of settling it.

### MS3 — Sidelines And Signals

- Focus: Kate
- Cast: Jayden, Mackenzie, Tracy, June, Dex, Martin on the field, Alex drifting between groups

- Stakes: Social control, emotional concealment, and whether Kate continues performing the version of herself that no longer fully fits.

The bleachers turn every glance into social language, the concession line creates awkward forced proximity, and Mackenzie keeps the air sharp without needing direct confrontation. Jayden gives Kate a stable public frame, but the more stable the frame looks, the more tension the scene can hold underneath it. Martin being on the field means he is both central and inaccessible, which is exactly the kind of distance that stirs unresolved feeling. Tracy and June, in different ways, register more of Kate's inner strain than she wants. The scene should not break her open, but it should make visible that her social fluency is no longer protecting her from emotional contradiction.
- DC: Medium
- MemoryTag: +sideline-tension
- Playtest Outcome: Partial. Kate holds public control, but the contradiction around Martin, Jayden, and her image intensifies underneath it.

- Focus: Alex

It is a deeper shared language between them and a stronger sense that the mystery belongs to the emotional architecture of the story, not just its plot spine.
- DC: Easy
- MemoryTag: +pattern-clue
- Playtest Outcome: Success. Alex and June turn the sketchbook clue into a pattern-reading method rather than a one-off discovery.
