# Grade_9 — Week_06

- Dates: 2023-10-02 → 2023-10-08
- Anchor: Prita's birthday and the Great Waterfall Bake-Off turn school systems and town rituals into a week about usefulness, visibility, and who gets to be known beyond their role.
- Week Summary: Week 6 should feel more grounded than the W.E.I.R.D week, but not smaller. The pressure comes from ordinary structures becoming emotionally revealing. Academic Pal's is no longer just an announcement. It starts creating repeated contact, obligation, and low-level intimacy. Prita's birthday adds a softer lens through which several characters can see her as more than a clean academic archetype. Then the Great Waterfall Bake-Off gives Waterfall one of its classic community stages, which matters because public town events always blur family identity, school reputation, and private feeling. Tracy's caretaker axis should be especially alive here because baking, service, and public warmth all overlap. Dex keeps dealing with what it means to be useful in a way people now actively seek out. June continues sorting real clues from projected meaning, while Kate and Martin both get a week where they are less trapped by their usual performance arenas and therefore a little more exposed as people.
- Calendar beats in play: Oct 2 Prita's Birthday; Oct 6 The Great Waterfall Bake-Off

## Week Flow

- Monday: Prita's birthday creates a small but meaningful social beat inside an otherwise normal school day.
- Tuesday: Academic Pal's starts producing actual study contact, awkward pairings, and small emotional readjustments.
- Wednesday: the band, schoolwork, and mystery threads compete for attention rather than exploding into conflict.
- Thursday: Bake-Off prep pulls Tracy and the town-service network into focus while others decide how involved they want to be.
- Friday: the Great Waterfall Bake-Off becomes the week's main public stage and forces multiple characters into visible roles.
- Saturday: fallout settles through smaller one-on-one conversations, especially around usefulness, admiration, and belonging.
- Sunday: the week ends with a quieter regrouping that points forward rather than resolving everything.

## Main Stories

### MS1 — Study In The Margins

- Day/Date: Mon 2023-10-02
- Location: Waterfall High library annex, courtyard bench, and after-school cafe table
- Focus: June
- Cast: Prita, Dex, Alex, one assigned Academic Pal pair rotating through, Nikole passing briefly
- Hook: Prita's birthday overlaps with the first week where Academic Pal's starts functioning as a real system, forcing June to spend time inside a structure she did not choose and pay closer attention to someone she had mostly read from a distance.
- Stakes: Trust, perception, and whether June can let organized systems produce meaningful contact without immediately treating them as artificial or suspect.
- Summary: The emotional point of this scene is not dramatic revelation. It is recalibration. June has been spending weeks reading hidden messages, social undercurrents, and institution-level discomfort, so this beat gives her a more ordinary kind of surprise: Prita is warmer, funnier, and more quietly self-aware than June expected. The birthday detail matters because it lowers the emotional armor around the day just enough for small observations to land. Dex naturally clicks with the practical side of the setup. Alex resists the school-designed vibe but stays because June does. Nikole's brief pass-through can add a flicker of self-consciousness without hijacking the scene. By the end, June should not feel converted into loving school systems, but she should accept that meaningful things can happen inside imposed structures too.
- DC: Medium
- MemoryTag: +prita-reframe
- Playtest Outcome: Success. June spends 1 Spark to stay open instead of withdrawing, which lets the Academic Pal setup become a real reframe of Prita rather than just another system to resist.

### MS2 — Useful, Therefore Wanted

- Day/Date: Tue 2023-10-03
- Location: computer lab, hallway outside guidance, and late rehearsal setup
- Focus: Dexter
- Cast: Prita, Ms. Vale, two classmates asking for help, Tracy, June
- Hook: After the W.E.I.R.D visit, Dex starts getting approached more openly for help, which looks flattering on the surface but risks pushing him back into an identity built entirely on usefulness.
- Stakes: Self-worth, social boundaries, and whether Dex can distinguish being appreciated from being functionally consumed.
- Summary: This is a Dex pressure scene disguised as a good day. Teachers are slightly more interested in him. Students are more willing to ask for his help. Prita understands the appeal of competence but can also see the trap in it. Tracy notices the moment Dex starts overcommitting before he notices it himself. June is grateful for what he can do but increasingly aware that she does not want him to disappear into support mode again. The scene should not punish Dex for being capable. Instead, it should let him feel the subtle exhaustion of becoming everyone's easiest solution. The right ending is not a meltdown. It is a small boundary or hesitation that shows he is beginning to learn the difference between connection and extraction.
- DC: Medium
- MemoryTag: +usefulness-boundary
- Playtest Outcome: Partial Success. Dex helps everyone who asks, but the win only counts because he finally hesitates before saying yes again and starts recognizing the cost of being everyone's easiest solution.

### MS3 — Sugar, Heat, And Labor

- Day/Date: Fri 2023-10-06
- Location: Mariposa Bakery kitchen, festival booth row, and judging lane at the Great Waterfall Bake-Off
- Focus: Tracy
- Cast: Diana, June, Dex, Aki stopping by, customers, two judges, Martin carrying supplies
- Hook: The Bake-Off puts Tracy inside the exact kind of public service-and-care role she excels at, but this time the event forces the question of whether she is participating as a helper, a daughter, or an artist in her own right.
- Stakes: Identity, exhaustion, family obligation, and whether Tracy can claim credit without feeling selfish.
- Summary: Tracy should be brilliant here, but brilliance is not the same thing as freedom. The bakery context means everyone already assumes she belongs in a support position, smoothing the event for other people. The interesting move is to let Tracy feel how much knowledge, taste, improvisation, and emotional labor she is actually contributing. June sees the artistry in Tracy's work more clearly than Tracy does. Dex respects the process in a way that makes Tracy feel observed rather than merely helped. Aki's presence reinforces the safety thread, but the event should also show why safety alone does not satisfy her anymore. Martin helping with carrying and logistics gives Tracy a rare view of him outside athlete pressure. The core win is not necessarily a ribbon. It is Tracy realizing that what she does has authorship.
- DC: Hard
- MemoryTag: +bakeoff-authorship
- Playtest Outcome: Success With Cost. Tracy spends 1 Spark to claim a baking decision as hers in public, earning visible recognition for her craft but also making the under-crediting at home impossible to ignore.

### MS4 — Looking Ordinary On Purpose

- Day/Date: Fri 2023-10-06
- Location: Bake-Off crowd lanes, waterfront rail, and a quieter side street afterward
- Focus: Kate
- Cast: Martin, Jayden appearing late, Mackenzie in passing, Tracy, Prita for one sharp exchange
- Hook: A town event with families, food, and low-stakes prestige removes Kate from the cleaner social scripts of school, leaving her less protected by image and more readable than usual.
- Stakes: Self-presentation, relational honesty, and whether Kate can tolerate being seen in a setting where polish matters less than presence.
- Summary: Kate is still good at events, but the Bake-Off is socially different from school prestige spaces. It is messier, more local, more family-coded, and therefore harder to dominate through style alone. She can still perform, but performance does less of the work for her here. Martin being present outside the field changes his texture in her eyes. Jayden arriving late lets the scene compare two different types of public fit without requiring melodrama. Prita can puncture Kate's reflexive certainty in one useful line or observation, especially if the earlier birthday/study beat has already started redefining who Prita is in the week's social ecology. The scene should leave Kate with a sharper awareness that she is tired of always curating herself, even if she is not yet ready to stop.
- DC: Medium
- MemoryTag: +unstructured-image
- Playtest Outcome: Partial. Kate stays socially graceful, but the looser town setting exposes how tired she is of curating herself and leaves Martin feeling more real to her than Jayden does in the moment.
