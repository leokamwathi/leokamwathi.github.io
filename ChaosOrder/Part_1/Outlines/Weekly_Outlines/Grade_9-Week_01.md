## Main Stories

### MS1 — Band Announcement

- Day/Date: Fri 2023-09-01
- Location: Waterfall High, music room 2B
- Focus: June
- Cast: Alex, Dex, Tracy, Ms. Vale, several freshmen
- Hook: Ms. Vale announces the Talent Show and pushes students to think in acts, duos, or bands instead of waiting for permission.
- Stakes: School visibility, creative control, and whether June gets to build a place that feels like hers before someone else defines her role for her.
- Summary: June reacts before she can retreat, which is the important character move here. When Ms. Vale frames the event in a way that rewards confidence and quick commitment, June publicly pushes for the possibility of a band rather than a safer solo path. Alex clocks the spark in her immediately, Dex sees structure where other people hear chaos, and Tracy's support arrives in the gentler way that makes it more stabilizing than loud praise. The outcome is not a complete band yet. The real outcome is that June creates a public opening and cannot fully disappear back into private creativity after that.
- DC: Medium
- MemoryTag: +band-formation
- Playtest Outcome: Success. June claims a visible role and secures the first real opening for the future band.

### MS2 — Echo Arrangement

- Day/Date: Fri 2023-09-01
- Location: Waterfall High, computer lab after school
- Focus: Dexter
- Cast: June, Prita, Ms. Vale, Finn drifting in late
- Hook: Dex offers to use Echo AI to build a rough arrangement and scheduling system that could make the possible band actually workable.
- Stakes: Trust, usefulness, ownership, and whether Dex becomes part of the social fabric or remains the guy who can solve problems without being welcomed into them.
- Summary: Dex's instinct is to contribute with competence, and the scene leans into both the strength and weakness of that. His arrangement demo solves a real practical problem: time, structure, and how to stop the idea of a band from collapsing under logistics before it begins. But Ms. Vale reads the output as technically impressive and emotionally thin, which is exactly the tension Dex needs right now. Prita respects what he built because it works. Finn needles him because competence makes an easy target. June, more importantly, starts to understand that Dex can become essential if he learns how to work with the emotional mess of people instead of trying to out-design it. The partial success here gives him footing without giving him ease.
- DC: Hard
- MemoryTag: +echo-help
- Playtest Outcome: Partial. Dex proves useful, but his method still feels too cold and keeps him slightly outside the group emotionally.

### MS3 — Image vs Image

- Day/Date: Sat 2023-09-02
- Location: Waterfall Town Square and nearby photo booth strip
- Focus: Kate
- Cast: Jayden, Mackenzie, Martin, a moving crowd of classmates
- Hook: A private text rumor starts to circulate while Kate is stuck inside a very public weekend scene where everyone is already reading posture, proximity, and status.
- Stakes: Reputation, emotional control, and whether Kate protects the performance of being fine or risks showing actual hurt in front of people who may weaponize it.
- Summary: Kate enters this story trying to manage optics and leaves it realizing that image management does not stop a shift in social air once it begins. The setting matters: the town square makes everything feel visible, the photo booth energy turns moments into artifacts, and Mackenzie's presence ensures every reaction feels observed. Jayden is useful but not fully grounding. Martin's attention is harder to read, which makes it more destabilizing. Kate chooses style and control over confession, which is true to her and still counts as a narrative loss. She survives the scene looking polished, but the rumor lands anyway, and the emotional consequence is isolation rather than collapse. That distinction is important for later weeks.
- DC: Medium
- MemoryTag: +rumor
- Playtest Outcome: Fail. Kate keeps her poise, but the rumor still lands and leaves her socially more isolated.

### MS4 — Note Left at the Lighthouse

- Day/Date: Sun 2023-09-03
- Location: Lighthouse overlook and stairwell
- Focus: Alex
- Cast: June, Nikole in the distance, unnamed passerby
- Hook: Alex finds a folded note tucked near a place June uses, and the language sounds connected to her uncle's letters rather than like a random prank.
- Stakes: Trust, privacy, social leverage, and the opening move of a mystery that could either deepen intimacy or create spectacle.
- Summary: Alex gets a choice that matters because it is small, private, and easy to mishandle. He could turn the note into attention and use it to pull himself closer to the center of the group's developing dynamics, but instead he does the harder and more generous thing by keeping it quiet until he can show June directly. That decision becomes the real emotional action of the scene. It also quietly links him to June's deeper thread before most of the others even know that thread exists. Nikole's distant presence helps reinforce that June's inner life is already being witnessed by more than one person, even if not all of them understand it the same way.
- DC: Easy
- MemoryTag: +uncle-letter
- Playtest Outcome: Success. Alex protects the clue and becomes a trusted keeper of June's deeper thread.

## Sub Stories

### SS1 — Bakery Rush

- Day/Date: Sat 2023-09-02
- Location: Mariposa Bakery, front counter and kitchen pass
- Focus: Tracy
- Cast: Diana, Arthur, two demanding customers, Dex briefly stopping by
- Hook: A surprise order spike lands in the exact time window Tracy hoped to keep available for rehearsal planning.
- Stakes: Family reliability versus the first fragile chance to choose something for herself.
- Summary: Tracy handles the rush well because she is practiced at absorbing other people's needs without making the effort visible. That competence is useful, but it costs her time and emotional space she wanted to spend on the new band thread. Dex stopping by matters because he sees a version of Tracy that is not just warm and easy, but skilled, strained, and working. The story is small on the surface and very important underneath: it establishes that Tracy's kindness is labor, not magic, and that future scheduling conflict around her will always carry emotional weight.
- MemoryTag: +bakery-conflict
- Playtest Outcome: Partial. Tracy keeps everything running, but loses rehearsal time and absorbs the stress alone.

### SS2 — Coach's Practice

- Day/Date: Sat 2023-09-02
- Location: Waterfall High, soccer field
- Focus: Martin
- Cast: Coach Alvarez, teammates, Malcolm on the edge of the drill
- Hook: Coach makes it clear that any split loyalty this early in the term will be noticed and remembered.
- Stakes: Team standing, paternal expectation by extension, and Martin's freedom to invest in interests that are not already approved for him.
- Summary: Martin stays at practice because the cost of not staying feels immediate and legible, while the cost of leaving only registers emotionally later. That makes the choice believable and a little sad. Malcolm's presence adds pressure without requiring a direct conflict, and Coach Alvarez embodies the system Martin already knows how to obey. The result is not dramatic rebellion. The result is friction building in silence, which is exactly the kind of thing that should pay off later.
- MemoryTag: +coach-conflict
- Playtest Outcome: Partial. Martin does what is expected, but the resentment and guilt follow him into the band's early formation.
