# Grade_9 — Week_02

- Dates: 2023-09-04 → 2023-09-10
- Anchor: Founder's Day turns private tension into public pressure, and the band has to become real faster than anyone wanted.
- Week Summary: Week 2 is about early momentum meeting real-world friction. The six are no longer just a possible group in theory; teachers, classmates, schedules, and the town itself start treating them like something that either exists or fails. June has to translate instinct into a real concept, Dex has to make his usefulness feel human instead of clinical, Kate has to decide whether public embarrassment means retreat or reinvention, and Alex quietly pulls the lighthouse mystery one step deeper. Tracy and Martin continue to absorb the cost of obligations that look small from the outside but have major consequences for trust and timing. Founder's Day gives the whole week a public stage, which means every personal crack becomes more visible.
- Calendar beats in play: Sept 8 Founder's Day Festival; Sept 9 rooftop trio formation for June, Dex, and Alex.

## Week Flow

- Monday: the group has to pitch an act concept to keep the better rehearsal slot, which forces June and Dex into leadership before anyone has agreed on hierarchy.
- Tuesday and Wednesday: schedule strain, rumor fallout, and side obligations hit Tracy, Martin, and Kate from different directions.
- Thursday: Alex follows the note thread into local history, while June starts realizing the mystery and the band may be emotionally linked for her.
- Friday: Founder's Day puts everyone in the same public space, raising the stakes on image, chemistry, and performance.
- Saturday: the emotional afterglow of Founder's Day shifts into a quieter rooftop bond where Dex, June, and Alex start functioning like a trio instead of loose overlap.
- Weekend: the group regroups after the festival and leaves the week with a clearer shape, but not peace.

## Main Stories

### MS1 — Monday Pitch

- Day/Date: Mon 2023-09-04
- Location: Waterfall High, music room 2B before first period
- Focus: June
- Cast: Dex, Alex, Tracy, Ms. Vale, Kate arriving late, Martin slipping in at the end
- Hook: Ms. Vale will give the group the better rehearsal slot only if they can explain who they are, not just list instruments.
- Stakes: The better rehearsal slot, the early identity of the band, and whether June can lead without feeling exposed or controlling.
- Summary: June walks into the room with instinct but not language, which means the scene becomes less about genius and more about articulation under pressure. Dex tries to turn the pitch into a clean system, Alex keeps pushing for mood over order, and Tracy quietly helps translate between people who are all right in different ways. Kate arrives late enough to make the room tense, and Martin's late entry reinforces that this group is already battling timing before it has music. June ultimately lands on an identity built around contrast rather than polish: something raw, layered, and not easily boxed. Ms. Vale does not fully love the vagueness, but she respects that the group finally sounds like a real act instead of six students sharing air.
- DC: Medium
- MemoryTag: +act-concept
- Playtest Outcome: Success. June secures the rehearsal slot and gives the group a real, if still emotionally messy, identity.

### MS2 — Echo, But Human

- Day/Date: Tue 2023-09-05
- Location: Waterfall High, computer lab and courtyard benches
- Focus: Dexter
- Cast: Prita, Tracy, Finn, Ms. Vale briefly checking in
- Hook: Dex gets one more chance to revise Echo's arrangement tools so they feel emotionally responsive rather than technically correct.
- Stakes: Credibility, trust inside the group, and whether Dex learns to design with people instead of around them.
- Summary: Dex approaches the problem like an engineer first, but the revision only begins to work once Tracy gives him language for why the old version felt cold. Prita helps him refine the logic without mocking the emotional problem, which matters because it gives Dex a way to stay competent without becoming defensive. Finn keeps needling from the edges, not enough to derail the scene, but enough to keep Dex from relaxing into comfort. By the end, Dex produces something better: still precise, but with room for imperfection, pauses, and actual human timing. The success is partial by design. He solves the immediate problem, but he also realizes that making space for other people may be harder than writing any tool.
- DC: Hard
- MemoryTag: +echo-revision
- Playtest Outcome: Partial. Dex improves the tool, but only after accepting that design has to make room for people, not just efficiency.

### MS3 — Public Smile, Private Damage

- Day/Date: Wed 2023-09-06
- Location: Waterfall High, lunch courtyard and girls' restroom mirror line
- Focus: Kate
- Cast: Jayden, Mackenzie, two unnamed classmates, Martin passing through, Tracy noticing more than she says
- Hook: The rumor from Week 1 mutates into a more casual, meaner version, which is worse because nobody treats it like a major event anymore.
- Stakes: Kate's social position, her ability to stay composed, and whether she lets someone see the cost of holding herself together.
- Summary: Kate knows how to survive an obvious attack. What throws her is the smaller cruelty of whispers that now feel normal. The courtyard sequence keeps everything public enough to be performative, while the restroom mirror beat gives the scene its real emotional center: the moment where maintaining the image stops feeling glamorous and starts feeling lonely. Jayden tries to help in the way someone helps when they want the problem resolved quickly, not necessarily understood deeply. Mackenzie does not need to openly strike to keep the pressure alive. Martin's presence matters precisely because he sees too much and says too little. Kate gets through the day without breaking in public, but she loses another layer of certainty, which may be more narratively useful than a dramatic collapse.
- DC: Medium
- MemoryTag: +rumor-fallout
- Playtest Outcome: Partial/Fail. Kate stays composed, but the rumor becomes more embedded in school life and leaves her lonelier.

### MS4 — Founder’s Archive

- Day/Date: Thu 2023-09-07
- Location: Waterfall Historical Society room and lighthouse path at dusk
- Focus: Alex
- Cast: June, Nikole briefly, elderly volunteer archivist, one passing town tourist
- Hook: A phrase from the anonymous note matches language in a Founder's Day historical display, suggesting the clue may be rooted in local history rather than just family memory.
- Stakes: Privacy, trust with June, and whether the mystery expands into something the town can touch.
- Summary: Alex and June follow the note carefully rather than dramatically, which helps the scene feel intimate instead of plot-heavy. The archivist provides just enough information to complicate things without solving anything. Nikole's brief presence adds emotional triangulation without making the scene romantic. By the time Alex and June end up on the lighthouse path at dusk, the clue has done what the best Week 2 mystery beat should do: it has widened the world, deepened trust, and raised the possibility that June's uncle was connected to something older in Waterfall than she assumed.
- DC: Easy
- MemoryTag: +founders-clue
- Playtest Outcome: Success. Alex and June turn the note into a real local-history thread without exposing it to the whole group.
