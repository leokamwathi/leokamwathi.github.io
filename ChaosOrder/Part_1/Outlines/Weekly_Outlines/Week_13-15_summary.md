## Arc Summary

- Thanksgiving-break intimacy begins turning back toward public structure once the Talent Show announcement arrives.
- The band enters a second-phase identity: same members, changed emotional realities.
- Martin's birthday and early December rehearsals prove that recognition, authorship, and tone all now matter differently than they did when the band first formed.
- June's private closeness with Nikole crosses into shared trio reality in Week 14, changing the social map by trust rather than by spectacle.
- The Holiday Bazaar then tests whether those newer truths can exist in public without becoming formal declarations.
- By Week 15, the ensemble is warmer on the surface, but less able to pretend it is unchanged underneath.

- June: moves from carrying private dual pressure to living truth more visibly, first through disclosure to the trio and then through quieter public tenderness around Nikole.

- Kate: moves from evidence into repeated choice, making her newer honesty feel more deliberate than accidental.
- Tracy: becomes more audible as an artist in rehearsal and more deliberate about care that does not erase her.
- Martin: learns more clearly that being centered is not the same thing as being known, and that his truest self keeps surfacing away from official performance roles.
- Alex: gets better at active restraint, then at letting art itself disclose truth instead of explanation doing all the work.
