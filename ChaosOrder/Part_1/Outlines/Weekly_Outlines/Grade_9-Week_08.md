### MS1 — Family Dinner From Hell

- Day/Date: Tue 2023-10-17
- Location: Lyu family dining room, kitchen threshold, and upstairs hallway landing
- Focus: June
- Cast: Grace Lyu, other family members at table, one aunt pressing too hard, Alex only by late text afterward
- Hook: A family dinner that should be routine turns into a concentrated argument about propriety, ambition, and the parts of June's life her family believes they still have the right to define.
- Stakes: June's privacy, family belonging, and whether she can defend her inner life without either detonating the room or disappearing inside it.
- Summary: This scene should earn the title without turning cartoonish. The pressure is specific, intimate, and old. June is not only being judged for visible choices. She is being interpreted through a family framework that keeps mistaking management for care. Grace and the older generation should feel believable: worried, proud, controlling, and not entirely wrong about everything. That is what makes the scene painful. June cannot use easy openness here. She has to decide whether to protect herself with silence, sharpness, or a carefully limited truth. Alex's later text matters because it gives the scene a soft landing without undoing the damage. The real result is not a victory at the table. It is June becoming clearer about how much of her identity work is also a battle over who gets to narrate her.
- DC: Hard
- MemoryTag: +lyu-dinner-pressure
- Playtest Outcome: Success With Cost. June spends 1 Spark to say one honest thing cleanly instead of either exploding or vanishing, which preserves a piece of self-definition but makes the family tension impossible to pretend away.
