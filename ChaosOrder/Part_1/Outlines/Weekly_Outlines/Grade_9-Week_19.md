# Grade_9 — Week_19

- Dates: 2024-01-01 → 2024-01-07
- Anchor: Winter break ends, and the first day back tests whether the changed defaults of late December can survive fluorescent light, public routine, and the school's urge to sort everyone back into old roles.
- Week Summary: Week 19 is a re-entry week, not a reset week. Most of the real movement happened over break, which means school now functions less like a source of change and more like a stress test for change that already exists. Tracy and Martin are on cooldown, so two of the ensemble's most embodied recent movers have to carry their new truths without force. That leaves June, Dex, Kate, and Alex with more room to define the public tone of the return. June and Nikole have to exist in normal school space after the calmer intimacy of late break. Dex has to prove he can reconnect relationally without turning first-day uncertainty into a systems problem. Kate discovers that public scripts are still available to her, but they no longer fit the same way. By the end of the week, the important result is that school does not successfully turn the group back into what it was before break.
- Calendar beats in play: Jan 5 Winter Break Ends

## Operational Snapshot

- Main Pressure: school re-entry tries to relabel everyone into older, easier roles just after break made those roles feel less true.
- Who Changes This Week: June, Kate, Dex, and Alex move most materially, while Nikole, Tracy, and Martin shape the week through steadiness, cooldown witness, and public contrast.
- Who Is Restricted This Week: Tracy and Martin are on Spark cooldown.
- What Advances: June/Nikole visibility under school light, Dex's relational re-entry, Kate's refusal to mistake polish for fit, Alex's non-managerial continuity, and the group's public reassembly.
- What Stays Unresolved: the admirer invitation, the Kate/Martin/Jayden decision, the deeper shoreline/W.E.I.R.D mystery field, and the larger family-pressure lanes waiting in June's birthday stretch.

## Continuity Guardrails

- Entering Week: Week 18 ended with changed defaults already in place; June carried quieter truth through family pressure, Martin chose the lower-pressure self again, Tracy learned to let care land without immediate repayment, Kate's polish-versus-feeling mismatch sharpened, and Alex proved continuity can survive without a second intervention.
- Cannot Contradict: Jan 5 is the first day back from winter break; Tracy and Martin are on cooldown all week; Jan 8 pairings and Jan 12 June's birthday are next-week pressure, not this week's event; and school re-entry should read as stress test, not magical reset.
- Newly True By End: June and Nikole can survive ordinary student visibility without shrinking back into secrecy, Dex can reconnect without over-optimizing, Kate's break clarity survives first-day public scripts, Alex can help a room hold together without acting like its engine, and the group feels changed in school air, not just outside it.

## Week Flow

- Monday: the year turns, but the real pressure is not celebration; it is the looming return to hallways that might try to flatten what break clarified.
- Tuesday: messages and private dread make it obvious who fears school as exposure and who fears it as relapse.
- Wednesday: family routines start loosening, and the characters feel the difference between late-break intimacy and school's coming visibility.

- Focus: June

### MS3 — Old Script, New Wrongness

- Day/Date: Fri 2024-01-05
- Location: main corridor between classes, front steps after dismissal, and the text thread Kate does not answer right away
- Focus: Kate
- Cast: Jayden, Martin, Melissa briefly, Mackenzie in passing
- Hook: The first day back offers Kate an immediate return to the polished public script she used to move through automatically.
- Stakes: honesty, image, and whether Kate can keep break-born clarity alive once the social field starts rewarding old behavior again.
- Summary: Jayden still fits the image of what should work. The school still makes that script easy to inhabit. Martin still feels less polished and more real. None of this is a revelation anymore, which makes the scene harder rather than easier. Kate's task is not to confess. It is to stop cooperating so fully with a script she no longer believes in. The success condition is one outward choice that makes the old pattern less automatic.
- DC: Hard
- MemoryTag: +return-script-break
- Playtest Outcome: Success With Cost. Kate spends 1 Spark to break one easy slide back into the polished Jayden-script, but the outward choice makes the contradiction more public to herself and harder to hide comfortably.
- Carry Forward: Week 20 can now pressure Kate through active school space rather than only private clarity.

### MS4 — Back In The Room, Not At The Center

- Day/Date: Fri 2024-01-05
- Location: music room doorway after school, empty chairs still stacked from break, and the walk out to the lot
- Focus: Alex
- Cast: June, Dex, Nikole, Tracy, Martin, Gabriel by one dry observation
- Hook: The first band-adjacent overlap after break gives Alex a perfect chance to narrate, tune, and emotionally host the room back into itself.
- Stakes: continuity, trust, and whether Alex can help the ensemble hold shape without behaving like the sole keeper of its meaning.
- Summary: Return days create social static, and Alex knows exactly how to cut through it. That is the danger. He could make a joke, a speech, a riff, or a framing move that tells everyone how to feel about being back. Instead, he makes one smaller opening and lets the room complete itself. Gabriel's observation matters because it reminds Alex that precision is still stronger than performance. The scene works if the room leaves feeling held, not managed.
- DC: Medium
- MemoryTag: +reentry-tone-restraint
- Playtest Outcome: Success. Alex helps the room cohere with one light-touch move and then stops, proving that school return does not require him to become the manager of everyone's reconnection.
- Carry Forward: Alex enters Week 20 able to participate in Jan 8's pressure without narrating it into safety.
