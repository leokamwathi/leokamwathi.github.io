## Essence
In a tense study session, Tracy offers structure when Dexter shuts down. A haiku prompt cracks open what words won’t, shifting them from rivals to scaffolding partners.

## Main Story

### Essence
Formal constraint becomes an emotional key—precision as care.

### Beats (time‑bucketed)
- (Afternoon — Tue, 3:48 PM, Library study room): Phone buzzes: "Mom." Dexter flips it face‑down, jaw tight; work stalls. Tracy registers, says nothing. [Pressure]
- (Afternoon — 3:52 PM, Library): Dexter fumbles a kinetics derivation he normally nails. Tracy slides over a blank index card: "Try seventeen syllables. Just… what it feels like." [Offer]
- (Afternoon — 3:56 PM, Library): First attempt is jokey; she waits. He exhales, counts on fingers, writes: five / seven / five—about noise and held breath. The room gets quieter. [Crack]
- (Afternoon — 4:01 PM, Library): He reads it once, low. Tracy nods and mirrors back one concrete word he used. "Keep that." Procedure resumes, oddly easier. [Name → regulation]
- (Afternoon — 4:09 PM, Library): They set a rule: haiku before fix when stuck; no sharing beyond the pair. Pens tap in sync. [Scaffold]
- (Morning — Thu, 10:06 AM, Chemistry classroom): Follow‑up. Their lab write‑up is cleaner; margin holds a tiny 5‑7‑5 box. Respect sits where rivalry was. [Shift]
