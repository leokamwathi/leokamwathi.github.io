# Chapter 3: The Hearth

- Calendar Period: Sunday, September 1 (Morning–Evening)
- Special Events: Weekly meal‑prep Sunday; Patch mending day (personal ritual)
- Locations: Tracy’s kitchen; Neighborhood thrift/market stall; Tracy’s room/backpack station; Alex’s desk (subplot)
- POV Character: Tracy
- Characters (Main Story): Tracy; Dad (offstage text check‑ins); Neighbor kid at the stall (cameo)
- Items/Motifs in Play: Granola bar pass; Patch (compass‑star) and placement norms; Consent language (“No posts” / “Ask first”); Hearth warmth (cinnamon/oven heat); List‑making as care

## Essence
Tracy builds warmth on purpose—cooking, mending, and planning small kindnesses for the first school week. She writes guardrails around consent so her care lands as invitation, not pressure. The “Hearth” forms as an anchor others will feel later.

## Main Story

### Essence
Care as agency: Tracy turns anxiety into tangible prep and explicit boundaries, establishing a reliable cadence that can welcome others without swallowing them.

### Beats (time‑bucketed)
- (Morning — Sun, Sep 1, Tracy’s kitchen): Oats, honey, chopped nuts—Tracy presses granola bars into a pan while the oven warms the room. Timer set; she lines small paper sleeves with neat labels. [Why: pre‑care lowers Monday friction] [Motif → Granola bar pass → StoryBible 11.9 Motif Index]
- (Morning — Sun, Sep 1, Kitchen sink): Voice memo to self: “Offer, don’t insist. Ask first.” Steam fogs her glasses as she washes up; she sticks a note inside her planner: “No posts; ask first.” This anchors her consent rule to concrete behavior. [Continuity → Consent language]
- (Afternoon — Sun, Sep 1, Thrift/market stall): A compass‑star patch catches light in a bowl of odds‑and‑ends. She buys it, thinking, “Find your way, but warm.” Later she stitches it onto her backpack strap near the left shoulder—visible but not loud. [Why: orientation + quiet pride] [Continuity → Patch placement norms]
- (Afternoon — Sun, Sep 1, Kitchen table): Weekly list—“What I can do this week.” Items: bring extras; check in with lab partners; leave a box labeled ‘share’; text Dad back before 9. She boxes bars: some plain; some with dark‑chocolate drizzle labeled “share.” [Stakes: care vs. over‑functioning]
- (Evening — Sun, Sep 1, Room/backpack station): She packs a small “share bag” (bars, tea sachets, blank mini‑notes). Decision: offer to a new student if they look lost; accept no with grace. Hand lingers on the compass‑star; she breathes once and zips the bag. [Foreshadow → first passes in Ch7/Ch10]

### Key Character Moments
- Tracy: Chooses to translate worry into structured generosity and writes a clear consent boundary so care stays mutual. [Setup → Hearth role]
- Dad (offstage): Text check‑ins land as support, not control; Tracy replies on time, modeling the boundary she asks of others. [Continuity]
- Setting/Motif: Oven heat/cinnamon; compass‑star glint; labeled “share” box as portable hearth.
