# Chapter 01 — Wolf Stalker (Snappier blend)

Kate slipped into the coffee bar on Joe’s heels and pivoted a chair into the aisle like a gatesman.

[Target inbound.]

She stretched her legs just enough to slow him and flashed a grin. “Fancy sharing a slice of pecan pie? Two forks, twice the fun.”

[Did I just say that.]

Joe mumbled something evasive, stepped neatly over her legs, and aimed for the counter.

[Clean dodge. Ow.]

Kate dropped her bag into the opposite chair and propped her chin on her fist.

[It’s Kate. With a K.]

He scanned the menu, hair falling into his eyes, sleeves tugged over his hands.

[Focus. Conversation, not spectacle.]

Online, at least, he answered quickly. Two weeks of cheeky messages from an anonymous handle and he’d met her there—full sentences, jokes, the whole thing.

[Online brave. In person, jelly.]

It would be easier tonight at Shelley’s party—big house, friendly corners, less espresso hiss.

[One real minute. No babble. No bolt.]

A chair scraped. Sarah dropped beside her, blocking the view. “You owe me a coffee. Black, no sugar. And no floaty bits on the top.”

[Saved by caffeine. And her.]

Kate sprang up, tossed her magazine into Sarah’s lap. “Compatibility Quiz. Knock yourself out. I’ll be right back.”

[Coffee, recon, dignity. Two out of three?]

—

“Report,” Sarah said when Kate returned with two mugs and cookies.

“He’s still allergic to eye contact,” Kate said breezily. “BUT—I’ve heard something interesting. Guess who’s going to Shelley’s party?”

[Say Mark.]

“Shelley’s party, Shelley’s party,” Sarah groaned. “What’s so special about a dumb party?”

Kate dunked a cookie. “Clue: tall, scruffy, never seen without a guitar.”

Sarah crushed Kate’s fingers. “Mark? I thought he had a gig at North Street with the Judas Trees.”

“It’s been cancelled.”

“Why?”

[Do not say destiny. Smile.]

“How should I know?” Kate shrugged, rescuing a crumb raft. “The manager probably heard them rehearsing.”

Sarah slumped, recalculating her evening from ice‑cream and video to lipstick and noise.

[Wingwoman secured. Phone supervision required.]

Joe drifted past toward his table. Kate tracked him with the corner of her eye.

[Hands, behave. Knees, don’t bolt.]

“So,” Kate tried casually, “what do you think of him?”

“He’s into computers, right?” Sarah studied the retreating hoodie. “What does he talk about?”

“Who cares about conversation? D’you think he’s up for a snog?”

[Regret. I do care. I panicked.]

“Is that all you want him for? Lip suction?”

Kate laughed, reclaiming the quiz. “Relax. Harmless fun.”

[Not harmless. It’s cover.]

Sarah shucked off her jacket. “Any chance of that coffee? Like today?”

“Only if you’re paying,” Kate said, hand out.

[Wallet: cobwebs.]

“Yeah, right. And pigs might fly.”

They sipped. Kate leaned in, conspiratorial. “Okay, wardrobe for tonight. Mark plus Judes equals indie cool—with actual color.”

Sarah shot upright. “What am I going to wear?”

[Fifteen minutes. Sprint.]

“I’ve gotta go,” she said, grabbing her folder.

“What about PJ?” Kate called.

“She’s meeting us there. Eight‑thirty. Don’t be late.”

[8:30. Don’t choke.]
