# Chapter 01 — Wolf Stalker (Rewrite with bracketed thoughts)

Kate trailed Joe into the coffee bar, the steam and chatter folding around them. She slid into a chair near the counter and angled it toward the aisle.

[Okay, calm. Say something normal. Friendly. Not weird.]

As he moved past, she stretched her legs to nudge the small table just enough to slow him, then smiled.

“Fancy sharing a slice of pecan pie?” she ventured, airy and casual. “My treat.”

[Keep it light. No big deal if he says no. Totally fine. Breathing is a thing.]

Joe murmured something she didn’t catch, stepped neatly around her feet, and headed for the snack bar.

[Did he even hear me? That was… a dodge. Again.]

Kate dropped her bag onto the spare chair and folded her arms.

[Third time this week he’s gone quiet on me. He called me “Cody” twice. It’s Kate. It’s not that hard.]

From here, she could study him without staring: floppy hair shadowing his eyes, hands tucked in his sleeves while he scanned the menu.

[Not helping. Focus.]

The only place she ever got a full reply from him was online. For two weeks she’d been sneaking into the computer room between classes to send quick, teasing messages. He answered those.

[Anonymity makes me brave. In person… less brave.]

Still, tonight there was a party at Shelley’s. Big house. Plenty of corners to actually talk in, without a queue of people and a humming espresso machine.

[A conversation. That’s the goal. Not a grand gesture. Just… a real minute.]

A chair scraped beside her. Sarah dropped into it with a grin, blocking Kate’s view of the counter.

“You owe me a coffee,” Sarah declared. “Black, no sugar. And no floaty bits on the top.”

[Saved by Sarah. Also, yes, coffee is a plan.]

---

Kate sprang up, pitched her magazine at Sarah, and pointed toward the quiz. “It’s all yours. I may be gone some time.”

[Mission: coffee for Sarah, glimpse of Joe, minimal humiliation.]

Sarah turned, clocked Joe at the counter, and sighed. Mystery solved.

[She knows. Everyone knows. Subtle is not my core skill.]

Kate flicked her strawberry‑blonde hair and caught the hem of Joe’s jumper with two fingers, playful and insistent.

[Too much? Dial back. Smile softer.]

He eased away, the line shifting forward. Kate let go and stayed where she was.

[Okay. Not a tug. Not a chase. Try again later.]

Sarah watched, then buried herself in the magazine.

“So?” Kate slid back, empty‑handed. “What do you think of him?”

[Tell me I’m not mad. Or tell me I am. I’ll take either.]

“He’s into computers, right?” Sarah considered his retreating figure. “What does he talk about?”

Kate frowned. “Who cares about conversation? Do you think he’s up for a snog?”

[Instant regret. I care. I do. I just… panicked the sentence.]

“Is that all you want him for? Lip suction?”

“Relax,” Kate laughed, reclaiming the quiz. “It’s a bit of harmless fun.”

[It’s not harmless to my heart. Also: don’t say heart out loud.]

Fun was Kate’s specialty. She took it seriously.

[Fun = control. Control = I don’t get hurt. That’s the math, right?]

Sarah peeled off her jacket. “Any chance of that coffee? Like today?”

“Only if you’re paying.” Kate held out her hand.

[Please say yes. My wallet is a fossil.]

“Yeah, right. And pigs might fly.” Sarah smirked.

Kate returned with two mugs and cookies, triumphant.

[Barista pity discount achieved.]

“I heard something,” she sang. “Guess who’s going to Shelley’s party?”

[Say Mark. Please say Mark.]

“Shelley’s party, Shelley’s party,” Sarah groaned.

Kate dunked her cookie. “He’s tall, scruffy. Guitar. Always guitar.”

Sarah squeezed her fingers. “Mark?” she hissed, scanning the room.

[Yes. Yes. The universe finally sent a text back.]

“It’s been cancelled,” Kate said of the gig.

“Why?”

[Don’t say ‘because fate needs him at Shelley’s.’]

“How should I know?” Kate shrugged, rescuing soggy crumbs.

Sarah sagged, calculating plans. Party now beat ice‑cream later.

[She’s in. Good. I need backup. Emotional and logistical.]

PJ had been weird for weeks. Tight‑lipped about lads.

[She’ll show. She always shows.]

Sarah bolted upright. “What am I going to wear?” Indie cool, but brighter.

[Boots in fifteen minutes. We can sprint.]

“I’ve gotta go,” she said, grabbing her folder.

“What about PJ?” Kate called.

“She’s meeting us there. Eight‑thirty. Don’t be late.”

[Eight‑thirty. Target acquired. Conversation window: open.]
