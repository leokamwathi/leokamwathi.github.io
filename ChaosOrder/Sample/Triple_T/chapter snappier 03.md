# Chapter 03 — Dating Games (Snappier blend)

“Guess what I’ve got in my pocket?” Sarah hopped over Kate’s legs and perched on the coffee table, grinning.

[Act normal. Do not squeal.]

“The money you owe me?” PJ asked, hopeful.

[Dream on.] 

Sarah dangled a tiny scrap of paper. Kate yawned. “Shopping list?”

Sarah thumped her shin. “Love letter.”

Kate shot upright. “From Mark?”

[Form a sentence, Sarah.]

“Who else?” She waggled it. “But maybe you don’t want to hear—”

Kate snatched it. “Where’s the rest? This is a sneeze, not a letter.” She read, “‘Friday night in the bushes was amazing. Meet me outside college today, four o’clock. —Mark.’”

[Patchouli. Yep, that’s him.] 

“What were you two doing in the bushes? Bird‑watching?” Kate teased.

“Just talking,” Sarah said, stealing a bit of Kate’s bagel.

[Plus kissing. Minor detail.] 

“You’ve only just had breakfast,” Kate frowned, guarding her plate.

“Is there a law against looking stunning on a first date?” Sarah pressed the note to her nose.

[Free perfume.]

PJ rolled her eyes at Kate. “What about Joe? Officially an item yet?”

“I’m meeting him for pizza.”

“In a restaurant?” Sarah sat up. “He’ll have to talk. Words. Sentences.”

“Deep spiritual connection,” Kate deadpanned. “We don’t need words.”

“Just breath mints and saliva,” PJ said, tossing gum.

“Is pizza dull for a first date?” Kate sighed.

“Destiny,” Sarah said, fishing out a magazine. “Scorpio: ‘Venus is partying in your sign—sparks fly at a lunch or dinner date.’ See? Pizza = fate.”

“What’s mine?” PJ pointed. “Libra.”

Sarah read, “‘You love to share your feelings—’” she coughed, “‘But a Sag lad wants to know you too well. Caution. Confide in friends instead.’”

“Confide,” Kate echoed, tugging PJ’s sleeve. “Spill about Conrad. It’s written in the stars.”

“And I let you read my love letter,” Sarah said, grabbing PJ’s bag. “Details. Or hostage.”

PJ nipped the rest of Kate’s bagel. “Okay, important news.” She hugged them close and whispered, “Conrad isn’t a Sagittarian.” Then flipped over the chairs, snagged her bag, and bolted, giggling.

[Catch me.] 

Business Studies was chaos. Conrad was already in, sleepy in a soft jumper.

[Huggable. Stop it.] 

He brushed her arm as she sat. “Miss me?” he yawned.

“Since breakfast?” PJ pinched his leg. “I might’ve survived.”

[Barely.] 

He stretched an arm behind her chair. “Footie match tomorrow. Come watch me?”

“I might,” she shrugged.

[Ninety minutes of legs. Plasters ready.] 

Flashback: greasy spoon at eight a.m. He’d admired his reflection in a spoon. “Ever noticed how big your nose looks?”

“You know what they say,” she’d said. “Fat head, big nose.”

[He’s so vain. Deliciously vain.] 

He’d brought a present: a tiny plastic elephant on a shoelace. “Cost me a fortune.”

“Cereal packet,” she’d said.

“Hours to pick the lace,” he’d grinned, tying it on her wrist.

[Still wearing it. Don’t overthink.] 

—

Four o’clock. Outside college. Sarah added mascara in a car‑window reflection and reread her horoscope.

[Taurus: ‘Night of your life.’ Noted.] 

Mark was nineteen minutes late.

[Fashionably. Allegedly.] 

“Hey, Sarah.” He rolled up in an old blue convertible, hood down.

[Of course he did.] 

“Am I late?”

“I just got here,” she lied smoothly, climbing over the maroon leather. “Thought you’d gone without me.”

“Never,” he said, wicked smile. “Somewhere special.”

[Beach? Band thing?] 

They cruised past her Art mates. Perfect audience. Then pulled into the ice‑rink car park.

“This is it,” he beamed.

[Skates? Seriously?]

“Meeting the Judes inside?” she tried.

“Nope. Just us.” He squeezed her hand.

[Romance, but… chilly.] 

She laced boots slow, watching him show off on the ice. He was good—confident, smooth.

[Try not to face‑plant.] 

He took both her hands. “Your turn. I won’t let you fall.”

[He brings all first dates here. Physical, not creepy. Fine.] 

“You’re a natural,” he lied, catching her elbows.

“Liar,” she giggled, wobbling. Close enough not to care.

Later, top down through town, the Judes on tape, his voice warm over the wind.

“Why don’t you sing on stage?” she yelled.

“They won’t let me. Afraid of the competition,” he grinned.

[Right.] 

“Start your own band?”

“Nah. Rehearsing my songs tomorrow. Come listen.”

“I’ll bring my camera,” she said. 

“How are your ankles?” he shouted.

[Puffed like life rafts. Worth it.] 

—

Joe waited outside Pizza Palace, dimples on display. Kate crossed slow, letting him admire her new top.

“I hope those lips are ready,” she murmured, touching his face.

“I can’t promise,” he said. “Shall we go in?”

“What’s the rush?” She tugged him into a shadowed doorway and kissed him.

He kissed back—soft, sweet—

“Urgh! Joe!” She pushed him away. “Garlic?”

“Garlic bread at lunch…”

“Kissing is off the menu,” she sighed, fixing her lipstick.

[Okay. Talking, then. Scary option.] 

“So,” she said as they slid into a corner booth, “rumors. You, Janey, last bus?”

“Who told you that?” Joe flushed. “Don’t believe everything.”

[Classic dodge.] 

“Well, we need something to talk about.” She tucked in beside him, twirling his hair. “Tell me about Conrad and Mark.”

“Nothing,” he said, opening the menu.

[Plan B.] 

She slid off a sandal and tickled his ankle with her toes.

“Kate, what are you doing?” he hissed.

“Checking for a pulse,” she said, trying the other ankle.

[Alive. Good.] 

She ducked behind her menu. “Oh no.”

Mish. Broomstick hair, incoming.

“So… you two are an item?” Mish sat, stole a breadstick.

“Yeah, and three’s a crowd,” Kate said. “Finished being nosy?”

“Who’re you with?” Kate added. “An imaginary friend?”

“One of your exes,” Mish purred. “You’ve had so many.”

“You’re jealous I can get a date without a blindfold,” Kate smiled.

Mish blew out their candle. “Save it for fans.” She flounced off.

[Horoscope said sparks. Wrong target.] 

Group date, then. Kate had just the place.

—

Beach. Towels. Binoculars. “Wildlife study,” Kate claimed. Sarah should’ve known.

“Still playing volleyball?” Kate asked.

“Joe’s serving. Conrad’s moving—” Sarah snorted. “Top’s coming off.”

“Oh my God,” Kate crawled closer. “Let me see.”

PJ wedged between them.

[Conrad topless. Standard.] 

“Conrad’s so brown,” Kate sighed. “Joe’s pink. Mark’s lighthouse white.”

“Ballad yet?” Kate elbowed Sarah. “Or just dental research?”

“What do you think?” Sarah laughed.

Mark’s new songs at rehearsal hadn’t been ballads—Shock‑a‑holics, Head Shrinker, Aliens Shop at Spaceways.

[We’ll workshop ‘romance.’]

“Conrad won,” PJ reported, biting an apple. “Joe’s laughing. Thought he was quiet?” She jolted. “They’re coming. Where do I stash these?”

Kate buried the binoculars under her towel—barely in time.

“Hey, girls,” Mark collapsed into the sand. “What’s the joke?”

“You wouldn’t believe me,” Kate said, elbow hiding the binoculars.

“Anyone for ice‑cream?” Conrad loomed. “Joe lost, so he’s paying.”

“You’ve twisted my arm,” Sarah said, grabbing her shades.

Kate watched them head up the beach.

[With a body like that, who needs brains? Asking for PJ.] 

“You and Joe hit the new club?” Mark asked, dropping beside her. “What’s it like?”

“Wild,” she lied.

[He hated the music. His dancing… no comment.] 

“Has Sarah done your runes yet?” Kate asked.

“No. Does it hurt?” Mark said.

“That depends,” PJ said, winking. “On the size of the runes.”

“Does she always go on about dreams?” Mark asked.

“Always,” Kate grinned. “She says dreams unravel your deepest desires.”

“Mine don’t need much unraveling,” he muttered.

[We noticed.] 

He covered his face with his T‑shirt. “Wake me when it’s time to go.”

PJ flopped beside Kate. “You haven’t said much about Joe. You okay?”

“I guess,” Kate said.

[How do I say this?]

Joe was gorgeous, a great kisser. It had taken weeks to catch his eye. Now she had him…

[Do I even want him?]

“Kate?” PJ pressed. “What’s wrong?”

“Nothing,” Kate said, biting PJ’s apple and rolling onto her back. “Joe’s a real cutie.”

[But is he cute enough?]
