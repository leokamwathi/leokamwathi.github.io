# Chapter 02 — The Big Snog (Snappier blend)

PJ checked her lipstick in Shelley’s upstairs bathroom, ruffling her dark bob while bass thumped through the floor.

[Nine‑thirty. Where are they?]

If she waited for Kate and Sarah, the night would be over. She headed for the stairs, dodging couples welded together mid‑landing.

[Obstacle course: tongues and elbows.]

The kitchen reeked of disaster—two lads were microwaving their trainers.

[Abort. Popcorn corpse.]

Fresh air. She slipped onto the patio hunting for Shelley.

[Locate humans with a brain cell. One will do.]

A hand brushed her waist—Conrad, all grin and easy heat.

“Hey, PJ. Catch you later?”

“Later,” she said, calm.

[Later = yes. Do not sprint.]

He was trouble in shorts, and half the college knew it. PJ liked quieter boys.

[Except when I don’t.]

Twice a week in Business Studies they sat close enough for toothpaste.

[Phone number? Too easy. Make him earn it.]

She let him talk sport, laughed at the dad jokes, made sure he glimpsed her between classes.

[Close. Just out of reach.]

It worked—he noticed. She checked now and caught Mish instead.

[Of course. Radar witch.]

“So, Miss Sporty Pants,” Mish purred, eyes on Conrad, “sudden footie fan?”

PJ shrugged. “Think what you like.”

[Conrad is a secret. Not even K & S know. Keep it zipped.]

Back inside, Kate and Sarah finally appeared. Mish drifted past, sneering at Kate’s dress.

“I didn’t know this was fancy dress,” Mish cackled.

“So why’d you come as Dracula?” Kate shot back.

[Focus, PJ. No explosions.]

“Is Joe here yet?” Kate asked, smoothing a stray hair.

PJ pointed to the lounge. “Snacks first.”

[And then carnage.]


Kate paused in the doorway. The lounge was dark, crowded, perfect. Joe sat by the snacks in jeans and a plain long‑sleeve.

[Target reacquired.]

She slid onto the sofa, close.

“Great party,” she yelled over the beat.

“Not enough food,” he said, leaning in, hair out of his eyes.

[Snog later? Food first. Classic.] 

He dipped closer like he might say something sweet.

[Here it comes]

“Food hunt? You coming, Cody?”

She thumped his arm. “It’s Kate, Dumbo.”

[With a K. I will carve it in your brain.]


PJ left Sarah with the art crew upstairs and went looking for her “later.” The balcony off Shelley’s room gave a view of the whole glowing garden.

[Too late?]

“Looking for someone?”

She spun. “Conrad.”

[You actually came.]

He teased her about balloon basketball, moved closer like this was easy.

“Come watch me play. I’ll show you my slam dunk.”

[Sports chat. Nod. Smile. Learn later.]

A shiver. He shrugged off his denim jacket and settled it over her shoulders.

[Warm. Smells stupidly good.]

Before she could decide whether to mention her cold lips, his arm was around her and he kissed her—sure and quick, like a clean tackle.

[Hello there.]

He grinned against the chill. “Let’s go dance. Warm up.”

[Public? Eh. For you—fine.]


Back in the lounge, Kate kept Joe dancing. His shoulders softened, his hands found a rhythm.

[Green lights.]

She tugged his top, pulled him close. “How much longer do we have to smooch before you kiss me?” She brushed his mouth.

He hesitated, then kissed back. Warm, sudden, more than she’d expected.

[Finally.]

“My lips are numb,” he said, laughing. “But nice.”

[Good answer.]

“Mission accomplished?” PJ materialized, fingers laced with Conrad’s.

Kate steered Joe sideways to gawk and landed on Mish’s foot.

“Watch it, big bum,” Mish snapped.

“A mate of yours?” Joe asked.

Kate ignored it, eyes on PJ and Conrad.

[We will unpack that later.] 

She drifted back‑to‑back with PJ. “When did this start?”

“Later,” PJ teased. “Where’s Sarah?”

“I thought she was with you.”

—

Sarah sat on the patio’s edge, a mayonnaise comet streaking her new skirt and hair.

[Stylish. Love that for me.] 

Mark hadn’t shown. Her sixth sense had lied.

[So much for epic destiny.]

She headed for the fairy‑lit summer house. A shape ducked into shadow.

“Who’s there?”

Patchouli and chewing gum answered before his voice did.

“Sarah?” Mark. “Quick—close the door. I’m hiding.”

“Who from?” she whispered.

“Samantha. Ex.”

He sprawled with his bass across his lap—scruffy jeans, too‑small shirt.

[Poster boy. Literally.]

“Great poster last time,” he said, lazy grin. “Do another?”

“Sure,” she breathed. “Maybe I should watch you rehearse again.” She unzipped her jacket to show the new top.

[Art. Research. Totally research.] 

“Requests?” He patted the bass, teasing.

“Make something up. A love song.”

He grimaced, plucking uncertain notes. “Like—a ballad?”

[Aquarius, creative, big feelings. I did the star chart. Don’t judge me.] 

“Walk?” he asked suddenly.

There was a path behind the summer house under moon‑warped trees.

“This is spooky,” she whispered, edging closer.

[Stay cool.]

She reached for him—gone. “Mark?”

Leaves whispered. A hand caught her, drew her through the hedge.

“Gotcha,” he laughed, pinning her gently and kissing her—once, twice—slow and certain.

[Yes.]

He checked his watch mid‑smile. “Band meeting. I’m late.” Another quick kiss and he was gone, back through the bushes.

“Wait!” she called. No number.

[Come on.] 

By the time she reached the lawn, he had vanished into the house.

Great.

Now what? Ignore each other Monday? Pretend this never happened? Or start… something?

[Monday, hurry up.]
